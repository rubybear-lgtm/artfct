## Design Context

### Users
Developers and technical professionals. Devs are the core audience but the tool is simple enough that other professionals (designers, PMs, marketers) who occasionally deal with HTML files would also find it useful. The landing page should be approachable — technical personality without a "no entry" sign for non-devs.

### Brand Personality
Bold · Confident · Fast. artfct (artifact without vowels) is a precision tool — not a SaaS platform. The brand should feel like something a great engineer built for themselves and decided to share. Simple to the point of being obvious: you drop an HTML file, you get a link. That's it.

**Voice**: Direct, terse, a little dry. Think: good README energy with personality.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Retrofuturistic** — the warmth of old-school CLI tools meets modern sensibility. References: the ASCII art interfaces of Gemini CLI, Codex, Cursor, Claude Code. Familiar to devs, novel enough to be memorable to everyone else.

**Color palette**: Solarized Light.
- Background: `#FDF6E3` (base3 — warm cream)
- Background highlights: `#EEE8D5` (base2)
- Secondary text: `#93A1A1` (base1)
- Body text: `#657B83` (base0) / `#586E75` (base00)
- Dark elements: `#073642` (base03)
- **Accent colors for gradients**: Yellow `#B58900`, Orange `#CB4B16`, Red `#DC322F`, Magenta `#D33682`, Violet `#6C71C4`, Blue `#268BD2`, Cyan `#2AA198`, Green `#859900`

**ASCII art hero**: Large bold ASCII letterforms spelling "artfct" (or a tagline), with each character or column colored using randomized-but-complementary picks from the solarized accent palette. Gradients across the letterforms — warm-to-cool or analogous combos, not random noise.

**Typography**: Instrument Sans for UI text. Monospace stack for ASCII art, IDs, and code output.

**Anti-references**: Dark hacker aesthetic (this is solarized *light*), gradient blob SaaS pages, cold corporate UIs, anything that requires explanation.

### Design Principles

1. **The page is the product** — The landing page doesn't market the tool, it IS the tool. Drop a file, get a link. The upload element is the hero.
2. **ASCII art as brand voice** — Not decoration. The ASCII letterforms are the identity. They should feel crafted, not generated.
3. **Solarized warmth** — The palette is warm and scholarly. Lean into it: cream backgrounds, rich accent pops, nothing harsh.
4. **Gradient as personality** — The randomized color gradient on the ASCII art is the one place to be expressive. Everywhere else: restraint.
5. **Approachable precision** — Technical enough to be credible to devs, simple enough that a non-dev isn't intimidated. One input. One button. Done.

### Product Decisions
- All links are `secure` and `ephemeral` by default. No tier selection on the landing page — this simplicity is the feature.
- Default TTL: 60 minutes. Surface as a trust signal: `ephemeral by default` in the footer.
